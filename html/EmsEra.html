A Fashion Collection

Type: Individual work

When: 2024

Duration: Four weeks, half time

Project scope: School project in Fashion Sketching & Illustration

This project focused on creating a full fashion collection, from research and moodboard to final sketches and a technical drawing. Ems Era is a modern autumn collection for women, inspired by earthy tones like deep brown, burgundy, beige and black. The aim was to explore my own illustration style and work with colour, material and form. I drew inspiration from COS, Acne Studios and Magda Butry by combining their minimalism, creativity and femininity. The process ranged from sketching to digital illustration in Adobe Illustrator.

Moodboard

The moodboard takes inspiration from COS, ARAKII and Magda Butrym. The aim was to mix the aesthetics trait of each brand into a mple visual direction. The result was an autumn atmosphere with earthy colours, natural light and materials like suede, wool and faux fur. The images brings calmness, confidence and elegance, with a balance between soft and structured elements.

Key words

The keywords were chosen after research and moodboard creation. They capture the balance between COS’s minimalism, Acne Studios’ creative edge and Magda Butrym’s feminine sophistication. Together they described the overall direction of the collection: minimalistic, earthy, sophisticated, feminine and contemporary.

The Line-up

I sketched all the garments digitally in Adobe Illustrator, which allowed me to work precisely with proportions, shape and details. The final line-up shows seven coordinated looks that form a cohesive story including structured coats, soft knits, tailored trousers and versatile dresses. Each piece was designed to be simple but expressive by combining clean lines with texture and subtle accents

Colours and Materials

The colour palette reflects the calm and warmth of autumn, with white, off-white, grey-brown, light and dark brown, burgundy and black colours. The materials were chosen for their texture and feel. Soft and tactile fabrics were paired with structured surfaces to create depth and balance. This combination gave each piece a sense of comfort and quality while keeping the overall look simple and clear.

Technical Sketch

The technical sketch was created digitally in Adobe Illustrator to make every line clear and precise. It shows one of the key garments from both the front and back, including seams, stitching and small construction details. I also explored the design in three different colour variations to see how the shape and mood changed with each tone. The drawing connects the creative part of the process with the practical side of making clothes and by doing that, turning an idea into something that could actually be produced.

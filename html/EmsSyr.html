An accessible webshop for handmade items

Type: Individual work

When: 2025

Duration: Three weeks, half time

Project scope: School project in Graphic Design, Universal Design of Digital Accessibility

The objective of this project was to design an accessible e-commerce website for handmade products. Several of the items showcased in the prototype, such as toiletry bags and even a headboard, are pieces I have crafted myself, making the project both personal and practice-driven. The project explored how accessibility and usability principles can be applied to create an inclusive shopping experience. The design process followed an iterative approach with interviews, user testing, and accessibility evaluation using the Swedish Agency for Digital Government’s self-assessment. A Figma prototype was developed to illustrate the website’s structure, navigation, and an accessibility menu that allows users to customise their experience through options such as text size, contrast and readable fonts. The project focused on clarity, simplicity, and equal access for all users.

Accessibility menu

The accessibility menu was designed to give users direct control over their browsing experience, offering options such as increased text size, dyslexia-friendly mode, adjusted spacing, high-contrast modes, grayscale, and the ability to hide images for improved focus. Although not fully functional in the prototype, the menu illustrates how users with different needs can customise the interface to support readability, comfort, and reduced cognitive load.

Alternative text

The project also emphasised the importance of meaningful alt text for all images. Product images were annotated with clear, descriptive alt text to ensure that users relying on screen readers can understand key visual information such as material, colour, and shape. In a complete implementation, all decorative and functional images would be handled according to accessibility best practices—either described in detail when relevant or marked as decorative when they do not add essential content. This approach supports an inclusive experience for users with visual impairments and aligns with established web accessibility standards.

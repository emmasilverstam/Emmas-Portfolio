An accessible webshop for handmade items

Type: Individual work

When: 2025

Duration: Three weeks, half time

Project scope: School project in ...n

The objective of this project was to design an interactive storytelling app for children aged 5–8. The project explored how AI can support children’s creativity in an ethical and responsible way, as part of the course Responsible Interaction Design. A Figma prototype was created to visualise the app’s interface and interactions, focusing on accessibility, inclusivity, and playful exploration.

Accessibility menu

When opening Taleable for the first time, the child creates a new profile by entering their name and choosing a preferred language. Next, they are invited to create an AI companion, a character that will guide them through their storytelling journey. The child can either draw their own companion or let the app generate one at random. Allowing the child to create their own AI companion makes the experience more personal, engaging, and playful. In Taleable, the setup intentionally leaves out gender selection, as it isn’t relevant to how the child interacts with the app.

Alternative text

The storytelling journey begins with creating characters. Taleable offers four playful ways to start: building a custom character, choosing from pre-made ones, drawing your own, or scanning a soft toy. When creating their own character, children select a base character by age instead of gender, allowing them to express themselves freely through customisation rather than fixed categories. They can personalise details such as clothes, hairstyles and facial features, or use the randomise button for inspiration. To promote fairness and representation, skin colour is randomly assigned. This reinforces the idea that all skin tones are equally natural and ensures diversity without making appearance a defining factor in the story.

Create Story

To encourage healthy screen habits, Taleable gently locks after 30 minutes of use, taking a 15-minute break. During this time, the app suggests real-world activities, helping children balance digital play with time offline. When it’s time to create a story, the child interacts with the AI through a simple chat interface. They can read or listen to what the AI says, and respond by typing or speaking, whatever feels most natural. As the child shares ideas, the AI repeats them and shows illustrated scene suggestions. The child can choose, adjust, or skip these, keeping full control while still getting gentle support. The AI guides the process, but the story always belongs to the child.

My Story

From their profile, the child can open My Stories to revisit or edit their creations. When a story feels complete, they select Create Story to finalise it. They can then read or listen to their story while watching the images unfold, moving through each page at their own pace. The illustrations remain still to keep the focus on the narrative, but with a two-finger press, they gently come to life with subtle motion. At the end of each story, Taleable encourages real-world play inspired by the child’s own creation, for example, continuing an adventure with a scanned soft toy. In this way, the app naturally connects digital storytelling with imagination beyond the screen.
